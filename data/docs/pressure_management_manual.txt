WATERWORKS OPERATIONS MANUAL — CHAPTER 4: PRESSURE MANAGEMENT
Document ID: WOM-CH4-2023
Revision: 3.1 | Date: March 2023

4.1 OVERVIEW
--------------
Pressure management is one of the most critical aspects of water distribution network
operations. Proper pressure control minimises pipe bursts, reduces leakage, extends
infrastructure lifespan, and maintains consistent service delivery to consumers.

4.2 OPERATING PRESSURE THRESHOLDS
------------------------------------
The following pressure limits apply to all zones in the distribution network:

  Minimum service pressure (consumer tap):    1.5 bar (150 kPa)
  Maximum service pressure (consumer tap):    6.0 bar (600 kPa)
  Normal operating range (main distribution): 3.0 – 5.0 bar
  High-pressure alarm threshold:              6.5 bar
  Emergency shutdown threshold:               7.5 bar
  Low-pressure alarm threshold:               1.0 bar

Deviations from the normal operating range must be logged in the SCADA system
and investigated within 4 hours.

4.3 PRESSURE REDUCING VALVES (PRVs)
--------------------------------------
PRVs regulate pressure at zone boundaries. Each PRV installation requires:
  - Weekly visual inspection
  - Monthly pressure calibration check (±0.05 bar tolerance)
  - Annual replacement of diaphragm seals

If a PRV fails to regulate within tolerance, isolate the zone and escalate to the
maintenance team. Do not attempt field repair without an approved work order.

4.4 SURGE AND TRANSIENT CONTROL
----------------------------------
Water hammer events occur when flow velocity changes abruptly (e.g., rapid pump
start/stop or valve closure). Prevention measures:
  - Controlled pump ramp-up: minimum 30-second start sequence
  - Slow-closing actuator valves (close time ≥ 15 seconds for DN > 200mm)
  - Air release valves installed at high points in the network

Acceptable transient pressure spike: ≤ 1.5× operating pressure for < 2 seconds.

4.5 SENSOR AND TELEMETRY REQUIREMENTS
----------------------------------------
Pressure sensors must meet the following specifications:
  - Accuracy: ±0.25% full-scale
  - Resolution: 0.01 bar
  - Sampling interval: 15 seconds (operational), 1 second (event capture)
  - Communication protocol: MQTT over 4G/LTE with TLS 1.2 encryption
  - Data retention: 12 months on local logger, 36 months on cloud archive

All sensors must be calibrated every 6 months against a NIST-traceable reference.
Calibration records must be stored in the asset management system.

4.6 ALARM RESPONSE PROCEDURES
--------------------------------
  High pressure alarm (>6.5 bar):
    1. Acknowledge alarm in SCADA within 5 minutes
    2. Check upstream PRV settings
    3. Verify pump operating point
    4. Notify shift supervisor if not resolved within 20 minutes

  Low pressure alarm (<1.0 bar):
    1. Acknowledge alarm in SCADA within 5 minutes
    2. Check for downstream burst (correlate with flow anomaly alerts)
    3. Isolate affected zone if burst confirmed
    4. Issue consumer advisory if outage exceeds 30 minutes

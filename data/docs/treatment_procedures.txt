SECTION 1: CHLORINATION DOSING PROCEDURE
-------------------------------------------
Chlorination is the primary disinfection method used at this facility. The following
procedure must be followed for all chlorine dosing operations.

1.1 Target Residual Chlorine Levels
  - Post-treatment (plant outlet):     0.5 – 1.0 mg/L (free chlorine)
  - Distribution network entry point:  0.3 – 0.8 mg/L
  - Consumer tap (regulatory minimum): 0.1 mg/L
  - Maximum allowable (WHO guideline): 5.0 mg/L

1.2 Dosing Calculation
  Chlorine dose (mg/L) = Chlorine demand (mg/L) + Target residual (mg/L)
  Chlorine demand is determined by jar testing every 24 hours, or after
  significant raw water quality changes (turbidity > 5 NTU increase).

1.3 Step-by-Step Dosing Procedure
  Step 1: Verify chlorine analyser is calibrated (DPD colorimetric method).
  Step 2: Record raw water turbidity, pH, and temperature.
  Step 3: Calculate required dose using the formula in 1.2.
  Step 4: Set dosing pump flow rate on the SCADA HMI.
  Step 5: Confirm dose by sampling at the contact tank outlet after 30-minute
          contact time (CT value ≥ 15 mg·min/L for Giardia inactivation).
  Step 6: Log dose, residual, CT value, and operator name in the treatment log.

1.4 Chlorine Storage and Safety
  - Maximum on-site storage: 500 kg (liquid chlorine cylinders)
  - Storage temperature: 10°C – 25°C, away from direct sunlight
  - Required PPE: full-face respirator, chemical-resistant gloves, apron
  - Emergency shower and eyewash station must be functional at all times

SECTION 2: TURBIDITY METER CALIBRATION
-----------------------------------------
Turbidity sensors must be calibrated following this procedure to ensure accurate
water quality monitoring.

2.1 Calibration Frequency
  - Online (in-line) turbidity meters: every 7 days
  - Benchtop (laboratory) turbidimeters: every 30 days
  - After any sensor replacement or maintenance event

2.2 Required Standards and Equipment
  - Formazin primary standard: 4000 NTU (freshly prepared or certified AMCO-AEPA)
  - Working standards: 0.1, 1.0, 10, 100, 1000 NTU (prepared by dilution)
  - Calibration fluid: particle-free deionised water (< 0.05 NTU)
  - Temperature-controlled environment: 20°C ± 2°C

2.3 Calibration Steps
  Step 1: Rinse sensor and flow cell with deionised water. Dry externally.
  Step 2: Run zero-point calibration with deionised water. Accept if reading
          is < 0.05 NTU; otherwise clean optical surfaces and repeat.
  Step 3: Insert 1.0 NTU standard. Wait 60 seconds for stabilisation.
          Adjust span if reading deviates by > ±0.05 NTU.
  Step 4: Verify at 10 NTU standard. Acceptable range: 9.8 – 10.2 NTU.
  Step 5: Verify at 100 NTU standard. Acceptable range: 98.0 – 102.0 NTU.
  Step 6: Record all readings in the instrument calibration logbook.
          Attach certificate and sign off.

2.4 Out-of-Tolerance Action
  If any point fails the acceptance criteria:
  1. Do not use the sensor for compliance reporting.
  2. Switch to backup grab-sample turbidimeter.
  3. Notify the QA officer within 2 hours.
  4. Initiate a corrective action report (CAR) in the maintenance system.

SECTION 3: pH CONTROL IN TREATED WATER
-----------------------------------------
3.1 Target pH Range
  - Treated water (distribution):  7.2 – 7.8 (optimal for disinfection efficacy
    and corrosion control of metallic pipework)
  - Regulatory compliance range:   6.5 – 9.5

3.2 Adjustment Procedure
  pH too low (< 7.2): Dose sodium hydroxide (NaOH) solution at the post-filter stage.
  pH too high (> 7.8): Dose carbon dioxide (CO₂) via diffuser upstream of contact tank.

  Adjustment increments must not exceed 0.2 pH units per dosing step to avoid
  overshoot. Re-sample 15 minutes after each adjustment.

3.3 pH Sensor Calibration
  Calibrate pH electrodes every 48 hours using two-point calibration:
  - Buffer pH 7.00 (phosphate buffer, NIST-traceable)
  - Buffer pH 4.00 or pH 10.01 depending on expected range
  Replace electrode if slope falls below 95% or above 105% of theoretical.
